How mentor pay is calculated

Each mentor earns a share of the subscription revenue their mentees pay — and each monthly invoice is split across two calendar months. This page shows how.

The rule, in one breath

A mentee's invoice is paid out to their mentor at the mentor's rate (35% → 50% → 60% over the mentor's first three months of work, then 60%). But the payout is split by the day the invoice falls on, using a fixed 30-day month: the part of the month still to come is paid this month, and the part already elapsed rolls into next month.

elapsed = day ÷ 30  ·  paid this month = amount × (1 − elapsed) × rate  ·  rolls to next month = amount × elapsed × rate

Each invoice's two slices add back to $255 (= $425 × 60%), so the mentor is made whole across the two months — no catch-up needed. March is low and June is high only because they're the start and tail of the chain; a steady mentee in a routine month nets the full $255. The June payout exists even with no June invoice — it's the elapsed slice of May's invoice rolling forward.

Notes: the month length is a fixed 30 days (so the 12th = 40% elapsed, the 19th ≈ 63.3% elapsed; a day past the 30th counts as fully elapsed). Pay is figured on the amount billed; a mentee is credited to the coach who mentored them that month. This mirrors the dashboard's engine (lib/pay.ts).
